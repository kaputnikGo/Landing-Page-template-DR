Dear Reader,

Right now, interest rates in Australia are at their lowest point in 47 years.

The last time the cash rate hung around 2% was way back in 1968.

If controversial economist Phillip J. Anderson is correct, interest rates could remain at these historic lows — or lower — for the next 100 years.

That may sound like an outrageous call… until you see Phil’s track record for accurately predicting macro–economic events over the past decade:

  • As early as 2005, he predicted the US housing market would crash around 2008, bottom in 2010 and begin recovering in 2011. CORRECT.
  • In 2009, he told infamous Aussie property bear Steve Keen he was wrong and prices would not crash in Australia. In fact, they would boom. CORRECT.
  • And in 2013, he forecast US stocks would hit all–time highs even on worse economic news. CORRECT.
So, what does Phil’s interest rate prediction mean for you as an investor?

If you think you can rely on a savings account to fund your retirement, you need to URGENTLY re–think your long term investing strategy.

Traditional wealth safeguards like savings accounts and term deposits simply won’t work any longer. Inflation is eating away at every dollar you’ve got parked in a savings account… and the return on a term deposit has halved in four years.

To put that in perspective… for every dollar saved you’re now receiving 50% LESS interest than just a few years ago.

Could you afford to live on less than half your current income once you finish work?

I didn’t think so.

So what’s your alternative? Do you throw your savings into stocks? Maybe property is the best option to grow your wealth? Or should you start investing in precious metals?
